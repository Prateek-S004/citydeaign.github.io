SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

Target 11.2: Safe and Sustainable Transportation

Indicator 11.2.1: Proportion of population that has convenient access to public transport, by sex, age, and persons with disabilities.

  • Developing efficient and accessible public transportation systems to reduce reliance on private vehicles.
  • Ensuring public transport is affordable and safe for all, including vulnerable groups like women, children, and the elderly.
Target 11.4: Protect Cultural and Natural Heritage

Indicator 11.4.1: Total expenditure (public and private) per capita spent on the preservation, protection, and conservation of all cultural and natural heritage.

  • Strengthening efforts to conserve historic sites, landmarks, and natural heritage within urban areas.
  • Promoting cultural tourism while ensuring sustainable management of heritage sites.
Target 11.6: Waste Management and Air Quality

Indicator 11.6.1: Proportion of municipal solid waste collected and managed in controlled facilities out of total municipal waste generated.

  • Implementing effective waste disposal systems to reduce landfill overflow and enhance recycling rates.

Indicator 11.6.2: Annual mean levels of fine particulate matter (e.g., PM2.5 and PM10) in cities.

  • Improving urban air quality by reducing emissions from industries and vehicles.
  • Encouraging urban greenery and eco-friendly transportation options.
Target 11.7: Accessible Green and Public Spaces

Indicator 11.7.1: Average share of the built-up area of cities that is open space for public use for all, by sex, age, and persons with disabilities.

  • Designing inclusive parks and public spaces that are accessible to everyone, regardless of physical ability.

Indicator 11.7.2: Proportion of persons victimized in the previous 12 months in urban areas.

  • Enhancing urban security and safety through proper lighting, surveillance, and community policing.
Target 11.C: Financial Support for Sustainable Urban Development

Indicator 11.C.1: Proportion of financial support to least developed countries (LDCs) for the construction and retrofitting of sustainable, resilient, and resource-efficient buildings.

  • Encouraging investment in green infrastructure in cities, especially in developing regions.
  • Promoting technologies that make urban buildings more energy-efficient and eco-friendly.
